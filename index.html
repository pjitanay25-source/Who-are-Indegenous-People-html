Who are Indigenous Peoples?

Learning Packet 1

Introduction

Approximately six percent of the world's population is comprised of Indigenous Peoples. However, studies demonstrate that this minority population plays a crucial role in supporting environmental sustainability. In reality, they actively contribute to preserving 80% of the world's remaining biodiversity and own, occupy, or utilize a quarter of its surface. Additionally, they are the keepers of wisdom and skills about how to adapt to the natural environment. But how much we know about Indigenous Peoples? In this learning packet, you are expected to learn about the main ideas and/or factors of being "Indigenous" and their linkages concerning identifying "who is Indigenous". Finally, you are expected to develop a thorough awareness of Indigenous peoples and their customs in a respectful manner.

Key Definitions

  • Indigenous: Originating naturally in a particular place.
  • Self-determination: The right of a person or group to manage their own life and choices.
  • Self-identification: The act of identifying yourself as a particular kind of person.

Indigenous Peoples in the Philippines

According to Amnesty International, there are 476 million Indigenous Peoples living in more than 90 countries worldwide. They speak more than 4,000 languages and are members of more than 5,000 distinct groups. A little more than 5% of the world's population are Indigenous Peoples. Seventy percent of them reside in Asia. Despite having diverse traditions and customs, they share the same painful realities: being taken from their ancestral lands, not being allowed to practice their culture, being physically attacked, and being treated like second-class people. Indigenous Peoples (IPs) are often neglected and discriminated against in legal systems across the world, making them even more susceptible to abuse and violence. Indigenous human rights advocates who speak out are often targets of state-sponsored intimidation and violence. Additionally, people may be murdered and physically assaulted just for being a member of Indigenous communities. Indigenous Peoples have been accused of treason or terrorism for peacefully attempting to protect their cultural identity or exert authority over their ancestral territories, which are often rich in resources and biodiversity.

1. Northern Philippines

Includes the Igorot tribes (Bontoc, Ibaloi, Ifugao, Kalinga, etc.). Who built the Rice Terraces. They also covered a wide spectrum in terms of their integration and acculturation with lowland Christian and Muslim Filipinos. Native groups such as the Bukidnon in Mindanao, had intermarried with lowlanders for almost a century. Other groups such as the Kalinga in Luzon have remained isolated from lowland influence.

2. Southern Philippines

In the southern Philippines, upland and lowland tribal groups were concentrated on Mindanao and western Visayas, although there are several indigenous groups such as the Mangyan living in Mindoro.Lumad groups of Mindanao (Manobo, Bagobo, Mandaya, etc.) and the Sama tribes of Tawi-Tawi.

Generalization

Discussions in international conferences over the establishment of definitional criteria for Indigenous Peoples VS the unrestricted freedom of Indigenous self-identification have shown a contradiction in international law regarding the establishment of standards. Indigenous Peoples are now being defined from either a positivist or a constructivist perspective. There are an estimated 14 to 17 million Indigenous Peoples (IPs) in the Philippines who belong to 110 ethnolinguistic groupings. In acknowledgment of this variety and within the context of national unity and development, the Philippine Constitution demands official recognition, protection, promotion, and fulfillment of the rights of Indigenous Peoples.
